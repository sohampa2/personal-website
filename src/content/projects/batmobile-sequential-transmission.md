body: "Built for UIUC's ME 371 Robot Heptathlon, competing across five events — strength, speed, agility, weight, and size. The Batmobile-themed car features a custom 3-speed transmission with high torque (8.44:1), high speed (1:2), and reverse — all switched via a servo-actuated dog clutch system controlled by an IR remote. The chassis is laser-cut acrylic with weight-saving cutouts; all gears, clutches, and mounts were custom-designed and 3D printed in PLA. The full electronics package — battery, motor controller, and IR receiver — is tucked under the chassis to keep the profile low and center of mass over the rear drive wheels for maximum traction. Total build cost: $44. Full fatigue analysis on the transmission spring using the Modified Goodman method confirmed 1M+ cycle life under cyclic loading. FEA of the 3D-printed transmission wall showed a minimum safety factor of 12.7 under worst-case loading — the car climbing a 15° incline with a 3 kg payload."
